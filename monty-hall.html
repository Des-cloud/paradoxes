The Monty Hall Problem

Introduction

If you are familiar with the TV show, Let's Make A Deal, you will probably have heard of the name Monty Hall. He served as the original host of the show. The show led to the widespread study of this in the field of Probability and Statistics and thus got the problem named after him. The interesting thing about this problem is the difficulty people have with agreeing to the solution. Let's get right into it!
Imagine you are among three people of which one person will be selected as the winner of a prestigious award. You keep bugging the head of the committee wanting to know if you won. He gets tired and agrees to give you clue. He says he cannot say anything about your chances but mentions the name of one of your competitors who lost. You immediately start celebrating because you chances have increased from 1 in 3 to 1 in 2. But errmm...
