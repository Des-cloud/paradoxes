The Barber Paradox

Introduction

The barber paradox is one of the interesting paradoxes in the world of Mathematics. In the 1900s Bertrand Russell came up with this paradox. Although solutions do not exist for paradoxes, it is less obvious for the barber paradox than the others.
Is there an algorithm that can take any algorithm and determine whether the algorithm runs forever or halts to a finite value? Is a set is an element of itself? These kind of questions drove Bertrand Russell to come up with the barber's puzzle and its paradox.

The Problem

The Barber Paradox is stated simply as:
Suppose you are in a certain town where all the men are clean shaven. No single male in the city leaves his hair overgrown. Every man either shaves himself or is shaven by a barber. There is only one barber in the town, who is as you guessed, a male. He shaves all the men who do not shave themselves. Does the barber shave himself? From the face this problem might seem simple, but it leads to a very interesting paradox. Watch an animation of the paradox below.

Paradox

If the barber shaves himself, he is a member of the class of men who shave themselves. But no member of this class is shaved by the barber. Since the barber is shaved by the barber(himself) and all members shaved by the barber do not shave themselves, the barber does not shave himself.
