The Rich Guest Paradox

Introduction

This is one of the most interesting paradoxes I have come across. Read it for yourself and also wonder!

The Problem

There is a very poor, quaint, little town where everyone is in a huge debt with someone but has no money to pay it back.

Therefore, this paradox is basically describing how nothing had changed from the start, but along the way, debts were paid, with no money.

Did the debt really exist at all? How exactly did this work?

How!!

Although this paradox seems strange at the first read, it isn't at all. Although all the debtors have paid their debts off and are happy (especially the hotel manager), they also all lost some amount of money that was owed them. So if $100 was the initial deposit, then all the people who was involved in the transfer of the money, gained and lost $100 each, thus cancelling the effect.
